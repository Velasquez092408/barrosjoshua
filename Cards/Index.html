Paris

Paris, known as the "City of Light" due to its significant role in the Age of Enlightenment and extensive gas street lighting, is widely regarded as a major European city.

Japan

Japan is known for arts, unique sculptures, gardens, poetry, and over a dozen UNESCO World Heritage monuments, including its famous sushi export.

San Francisco

San Francisco is famous for the iconic Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz, buzzing neighborhoods such as Chinatown, and its many museums and historic architecture.

New York

New York City is known by various nicknames, including "the City that Never Sleeps" and "Gotham," but "the Big Apple" gained prominence in the 1920s, coined by John J. Penn.

South Korea

The Korean Wave is a phenomena that describes South Korea's famed pop culture in the twenty-first century, particularly in the areas of music (K-pop), TV dramas (K-dramas), and film.

Thailand

Thailand attracts 35 million tourists annually, known for its stunning southern islands, unique street food scene, and numerous temples, making it a popular travel destination.
